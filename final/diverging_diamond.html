The Diverging Diamond Interchange

About The Diverging Diamond Interchange

The diverging diamond interchange is a more advanced version of the diamond interchange. The non freeway road crosses lanes to get the turning lane closer to the exit ramp. The two crossroads on each end have turn signals that allow each end to go one at a time.

Common Locations of The Diverging Diamond Interchange

The diverging diamond interchange was first introduced in France in the 1970's but is now used all over the globe. The first one in the U.S. was in 2005 in the state of Ohio on interstate 75 and U.S Route 224. One local one is in Pigeon Forge, TN at the Interstate 75/40 and Highway 338.

Pros and Cons of The Diverging Diamond Interchange

There are a lot of pros as well as cons. One pro is the short signal cycle, creating more traffic flow. Another is the reduced number of cross traffic contact points. Some cons are some drivers may become confused by the opposite lane direction being flipped in the middle. Another is the amount of pedrestian crosswalks needed.
