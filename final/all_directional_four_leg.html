The All Directional Four Leg Interchange

About The All Directional Four Leg Interchange

The All Directional Four Leg Interchange is also known as a stack interchange. This multi level interchange is grade separated and has slip lanes that are stacked above each other. There are more 3 way, 5 way and a few 6 way variations of this.

Common Locations of The All Directional Four Leg Interchange

The first stack interchange was constructed on the intersection of U.S. Highway 101 and State route 110 in Los Angeles, California in 1949. There are many others in other counbtries such as Canada, Belgium, Germany, Greece, and the UK. There are many in the southern hemisphere as well.

Pros and Cons of The All Directional Four Leg Interchange

The all directional four leg interchange has many pros and a few cons. One pro is the ability to handle an insane amount of traffic volume and successfully keep the flow very fast. Another is the symetric and easy to follow design for motorists. One con is the sheer amount of land that is required to build one of these. Another con is the price. Building a 4 level interchange can cost cities millions of dollars.
