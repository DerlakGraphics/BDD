The Trumpet Interchange

About The Trumpet Interchange

The Trumpet Interchange is a three leg interchange that gets its name from the shape the slip lanes and exit ramps form. They are mostly used in rural areas off of interstates and motorways to keep the larger road's traffic at a steady flow.

Common Locations of The Trumpet Interchange

The first trumpet interchange was opened in 1959 in the United Kingdom. There are several examples of these in other areas of the world as well. One example is here in Chattanooga on the Dupont Parkway and Amnicola Highway close to campus. There are several other variants on more rural roads as well

Pros and Cons of The Trumpet Interchange

One pro of the Trumpet Interchange is the ability to have very miniumal bridges, therefore cutting the cost. Another is the ability to cut down on cross traffic. Another pro is the minimal land used by the footprint. One con is they cannot handle large amounts of traffic. Another con is the loop can cause rollovers if taken too fast.
