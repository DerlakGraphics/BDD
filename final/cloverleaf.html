The Cloverleaf Interchange

About The Cloverleaf Interchange

The Cloverleaf interchange is a two level interchange that is completely managed by slip turns. They get their name from the circular slip lanes forming the 4 leaves of a "cloverleaf". The slip lanes come off of the main roads that intersect.

Common Locations of Cloverleaf Interchange

The cloverleaf interchange is most commonly found in the United States. They were first introduced in mass quanity when the interstate system was being constructed in the 1940's. There are many configurations in larger cities in the U.S. The first one ever built was in Woodbridge Township, NJ, U.S.A. There are a few examples in the U.A.E, as well as Canada.

Pros and Cons of Cloverleaf Interchange

The cloverleaf interchange has many pros, as well as cons. It allows for a constant flow of traffic with no stops. It also is able to handle more traffic than the standard diamond variant. The cons, sometimes semi trucks will take the curves wey too fast and will overturn. Another is, the lane merging trait causes issues when there is a massive amount of traffic.
